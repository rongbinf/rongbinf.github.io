Wei Lan (Wave) Library is a real, non-government, non-profit organisation aimed at building libraries for floating children in China.

  • What's the background?
    • There are an estimated 292.51 million Source: National Bureau of Statistics, 2021 rural migrant workers in China's cities as delivery motorbike-riders, laborers, doorkeepers and at other jobs that urbanites avoid.
    • The children of this floating population are called floating children.
    • The families, holding a rural Hukou Hukou, China's household registration system, which divides population into two different statuses: agricultural and non-agricultural residency statuses. Yet China provides a wide range of public education, resources in cities, which means only city hukou holders could benefit from, are exceedingly more than schools in rural. and on a tight budget, could only afford low-cost private schools which barely have good education resources, even books.
    • The organisation is aimed at helping these children.
  • How?
    • Though some of these schools do receive donations, the lack of teacher and librarian hinders the daily operation of the school library.
    • Here, volunteers from Wei Lan Library take the responsibility by spending one or two days a week, sorting books, helping kids find good ones and maintaining a rather safe environment to study.
    • And they step even further: conduct both quantitative and qualitative research, reach out to publishing houses and enterprises about possibilities to donate, work with press and media, speak out at forums and conferences and so forth.
  • I'm interested in it! Can I be of help?
    • Yes! If you are based in China, you are very welcome to be a volunteer on-site to help with the daily operation - this is the key and the heart of the project.
    • If you'd like to support with public communication, promotion, information disclosure, or anything you can think of and willing to, you can always find a place here.
    • Read more about the project on its official WeChat account and official website (Simplified Chinese). Drop me a message if you don't speak Chinese, and I'm more than happy to help you with learning more about Wei Lan and also the recruitment process.

Why is having your own website important?

  1. Platforms are losing their credibility.
    Your accounts can be easily suspended and even deleted. A personal website offers a stable, independent platform, safeguarding your digital presence against these uncertainties.
  2. Your thoughts and ideas are precious to the world.
    If you don't take the full control (and also the "property") of them, they'll be vanished like dust. A personal website ensures that your intellectual contributions are permanently accessible and under your control.
  3. You can be eternal (sounds creepy but still) by having a website.
    Simply back it up on archive.org (let's assume this platform can last forever), or place your site on a device (e.g. a Raspberry Pi), save some money, and ask a bank or any institute that can keep the device on and online. This provide a form of digital immortality for future creatures to study our naive minds.

      • One of the best desktop Markdown editors, cheaper than iA Writer, one-time charges.
